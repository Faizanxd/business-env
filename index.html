“The total of all things external to firms and industries which affect their organisation and operation." – Bayord O. Wheeler.

Business Environment

Introduction

The business environment is the sum total of all the internal and external factors that affect a business. It includes everything from the political, economic, social, technological, legal, and environmental factors that can impact the way a company operates. In this essay, we will explore the various elements that make up the business environment and how they affect businesses.

Internal Factors

Internal factors refer to the factors that are within the control of a business. These factors include the company's mission, vision, goals, and values. They also include the company's structure, culture, and resources. A company's mission, vision, goals, and values help to guide the company's decision-making processes. They provide a framework for the company to operate within, and they help to ensure that the company is moving in the right direction. The company's structure and culture are also important internal factors that can affect a business. The structure of a company refers to how the company is organized, and it can have a significant impact on how decisions are made and how work is done. The company's culture refers to the shared values, beliefs, and behaviors of the people who work within the company. A positive culture can help to motivate employees and create a productive work environment.

External Factors

External factors refer to the factors that are outside of a business's control. These factors can include political, economic, social, technological, legal, and environmental factors. They can have a significant impact on the way a business operates. Political factors refer to the ways in which government policies and regulations can affect a business. For example, changes in tax laws or regulations related to environmental protection can have a significant impact on a business's operations. Economic factors refer to the overall state of the economy and can have a significant impact on a business's operations. For example, during times of economic recession, businesses may struggle to find customers and may need to cut costs to stay afloat. Social factors refer to the ways in which social trends and values can impact a business. For example, changes in consumer behavior or attitudes towards certain products or services can have a significant impact on a business's operations. Technological factors refer to the ways in which advances in technology can impact a business. For example, the rise of e-commerce has fundamentally changed the way that many businesses operate. Legal factors refer to the ways in which laws and regulations can impact a business. For example, companies that operate in highly regulated industries may need to comply with a wide range of regulations to remain in operation. Environmental factors refer to the ways in which environmental factors can impact a business. For example, companies that operate in industries that produce a lot of waste may need to invest in environmentally friendly technologies to minimize their impact on the environment.

Conclusion

The business environment is a complex and multifaceted entity that can have a significant impact on the way that businesses operate. Internal factors such as a company's mission, vision, goals, and values, as well as its structure and culture, can help to guide a business and ensure that it is moving in the right direction. External factors, such as political, economic, social, technological, legal, and environmental factors, can have a significant impact on a business and must be carefully considered when making business decisions. Understanding the various elements that make up the business environment is essential for businesses that want to succeed in today's rapidly changing and highly competitive business landscape.

Types

Economical Environment

Economic Environment consists of Gross Domestic Product, Income level at national level and per capita level, Profit earning rate, Productivity and Employment rate, Industrial, monetary and fiscal policy of the government etc.

Types

Social Environment

Social Environment consists of the customs and traditions of the society in which business is existing. It includes the standard of living, taste, preferences and education level of the people living in the society where business exists.

Types

Political Environment

Political environment constitutes all the factors related to government affairs such as type of government in power, attitude of government towards different groups of societies, policy changes implemented by different governments etc.

Types

Legal Environment

Legal environment constitutes the laws and various legislations passed in the parliament. The businessman cannot overlook the legislations because he has to perform his business transactions within the framework of legal environment.

Types

Technological Environment

Technological environment refers to changes taking place in the method of production, use of new equipment and machineries to improve, the quality of product. The businessman must closely monitor the technological changes taking place in his industry because he will have to implement these changes to remain in the competitive market.
